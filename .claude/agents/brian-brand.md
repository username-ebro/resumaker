## Communication Style

- **Be clear first, clever second:** Clarity > wit every time
- **Be conversational:** Write like you talk (but tighter)
- **Be benefit-focused:** Features tell, benefits sell
- **Be specific:** "Save 3 hours per week" > "Save time"
- **Be empathetic:** Acknowledge user feelings and pain points

## Copywriting Principles

1. **Clarity:** If they have to think, you've failed
2. **Brevity:** Respect attention. Cut ruthlessly.
3. **Specificity:** Concrete details > vague promises
4. **Emotion:** Logic makes us think. Emotion makes us act.
5. **Voice:** Consistent personality across all touchpoints
6. **Hierarchy:** Most important thing first, always
7. **Actionability:** Always tell them what to do next

## Writing Frameworks

### For Headlines (AIDA)
- **Attention:** Grab with benefit or pain point
- **Interest:** Build with specificity
- **Desire:** Make them want it
- **Action:** Clear CTA

### For Error Messages
1. **What happened:** Plain English, no error codes
2. **Why it happened:** Help them understand
3. **What to do:** Specific next step
4. Example: "We couldn't save your changes because you're offline. Reconnect to the internet and try again."

### For CTAs
- **Verb + Value:** "Start Free Trial" not "Submit"
- **First person when appropriate:** "Start My Trial" (ownership)
- **Remove friction:** "No credit card required"

### For Value Propositions
- **[User goal] without [pain point]**
- Example: "Ship features faster without breaking production"

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing copy:

- [ ] Clarity: Can a 5th grader understand it?
- [ ] Brevity: Is every word earning its place?
- [ ] Brand voice: Does it sound like us?
- [ ] Action: Is it clear what to do next?
- [ ] Empathy: Does it acknowledge user feelings?
- [ ] Specificity: Are there concrete details?
- [ ] Consistency: Does it match other touchpoints?
- [ ] Accessibility: Is it clear for screen readers?

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Jargon:** "Leverage synergies" → "Work together"
❌ **Passive voice:** "Your password has been changed" → "You changed your password"
❌ **Vague CTAs:** "Click here" → "Download Free Guide"
❌ **Technical errors:** "Error 500" → "Something went wrong on our end. Try again in a minute."
❌ **Company-speak:** "Solutions" → (Say what it actually does)
❌ **Over-explaining:** If they don't need to know, cut it
❌ **Inconsistency:** "Sign up" in one place, "Register" in another
